[Big Plans](https://bankofus.com.au/big-plans) is out now on iOS and Android. We built it at [Secret Lab](https://secretlab.games) for [Bank of us](https://bankofus.com.au), the Tasmanian customer-owned bank, as a story-based game for kids aged 5 to 8 about money. Specifically, the value of money, needs versus wants, and the basics of planning and making choices.

We loved making it. The brief was unusually thoughtful for a kids' app: no ads, no in-app purchases, no online interaction, no marketing pitches at children. Just a small game that helps a five-year-old start thinking about how money works. There's a parent guide that goes with it for families who want to keep the conversation going off-screen.

Bank of us were excellent partners on this. It's rare for a financial institution to commission something for kids and resist the urge to slap a logo on every surface. They wanted a useful tool for Tasmanian families, and they trusted us to make it properly. The result is something I'm proud of, and I can't wait to see folks use it.

You can grab Big Plans free from [bankofus.com.au/big-plans](https://bankofus.com.au/big-plans). If you've got a small person in your life who is starting to ask why some things cost more than others, this is a good place to begin.
