I'm really pleased to share that I've been [awarded a 2026 Libraries Tasmania Research Fellowship](https://www.premier.tas.gov.au/latest-news/2026/april/libraries-tasmania-2026-fellowships-announced). The project is on Tasmania's space history, from the establishment of [Rossbank Observatory](https://trove.nla.gov.au/search/category/newspapers?keyword=%22Rossbank+Observatory%22) in 1840 through to the contemporary work being done here on tracking interplanetary spacecraft. There's a surprisingly long thread connecting convict-era astronomy in Hobart to the antennas, satellites, and deep-space tracking that I talk about most months on [ABC Radio](/tags/space-news/), and I'm looking forward to spending some quality time in the [State Library and Archives](https://libraries.tas.gov.au/slat/fellowship/) pulling that thread.

A big thank you to Libraries Tasmania and to everyone who has supported the application. Tasmania's archives are genuinely world-class, and this kind of programme is a reminder of how lucky we are to have them.

Congratulations also to the other 2026 recipients: Liz Tew (Aboriginal Fellowship, examining colonial surveyor records from 1803 to 1840), Robert Jarman (Writing Fellowship, on the convict actor Alonzo Johnson), and Sam George-Allen (Writing Fellowship, forest-themed short stories). Looking forward to seeing all of this work emerge.

I'll write more as the research takes shape. There's a great deal more space in Tasmania's history than most people realise.
